Safe Social Media Practices

1. Protect Your Personal Information

  • Avoid sharing sensitive details like your full name, address, phone number, or school.
  • Limit the amount of personal data visible on your profile.
  • Be cautious when sharing your location, especially in real-time.

2. Strengthen Your Privacy Settings

  • Regularly review and update your privacy settings.
  • Adjust who can see your posts, profile information, and friend list.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for added security.

3. Be Cautious with Friend Requests

  • Only accept friend requests from people you know in real life.
  • Watch out for fake profiles and impersonators.
  • If unsure, verify the person through another channel before accepting.

4. Think Before You Post

  • Avoid sharing personal grievances, confidential information, or impulsive reactions.
  • Consider the long-term impact of your posts before publishing.
  • Remember that once something is posted, it can be difficult to remove completely.

5. Recognize and Avoid Scams

  • Be wary of messages or links promising prizes, freebies, or urgent actions.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments.
  • Report and block suspicious accounts.

6. Use Strong Passwords

  • Create unique, complex passwords for each social media account.
  • Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Change passwords regularly and avoid reusing old ones.

7. Verify Information Before Sharing

  • Cross-check news, images, or videos before resharing.
  • Rely on trusted sources and fact-checking websites.
  • Avoid spreading misinformation or unverified content.

8. Be Mindful of Cyberbullying and Online Harassment

  • Do not engage in negative or harmful conversations.
  • Report and block individuals who engage in harassment.
  • Offer support to those experiencing cyberbullying.

9. Secure Your Devices

  • Keep your devices updated with the latest security patches.
  • Install antivirus software to protect against malware and phishing attacks.
  • Lock your device with a password, PIN, or biometric authentication.

10. Teach and Encourage Safe Online Behavior

  • Educate younger users on responsible social media use.
  • Promote a culture of kindness and respect online.
  • Stay informed about new social media threats and updates.
